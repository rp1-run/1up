## Lean row grammar

Every `search` / `symbol` / `impact` row matches:

```
<score>  <path>:<l1>-<l2>  <kind>  <breadcrumb>::<symbol>  :<segment_id>[  ~<channel>]
```

- `:<segment_id>` is a 12-char handle. Feed it into `1up get` to read the body or `1up impact --from-segment <handle>` to expand from that exact anchor.
- `impact` rows carry a trailing `~P` (primary) or `~C` (contextual) tag. Primary rows are confident relation-backed callers; contextual rows are lower-signal and must be verified before acting on them.

Core commands (`search`, `get`, `symbol`, `impact`, `context`, `structural`) emit one machine-parseable shape and do not accept `--format`, `-f`, `--full`, `--brief`, `--human`, or `--verbose-fields`.

## Impact workflow

1. Find the anchor. If you already know the symbol or file, skip straight to step 2. Otherwise run `1up search "<intent>" -n 3` and pick the 1-2 most relevant handles.
2. Expand blast radius:
   - `1up impact --from-segment <handle>` from a search/symbol row.
   - `1up impact --from-symbol <name>` from a known symbol.
   - `1up impact --from-file <path>` when the anchor is a file.
3. Read primary (`~P`) rows first; hydrate bodies with `1up get :<handle>` when you need more than the header.
4. Cover the known misses: `impact` may not surface UI callers, dynamic dispatch, or indirect consumers. Follow up with `1up symbol -r <name>` for reference lists, and only then reach for `grep` to confirm a specific literal.

### Batching

- One call, many handles: `1up get :h1 :h2 :h3`.
- Chain w/ `&&`: `1up impact --from-symbol Foo && 1up symbol Foo -r` → one turn, two result sets.
- One wider search (`-n 5..8`) beats several narrow searches on adjacent concepts.

### Use `1up symbol`, not `1up get | grep`

Piping `1up get` through `grep` tokenizes the full body then filters client-side — wasteful. Use `1up symbol <name>` (`-r` for refs, `--fuzzy` for approximate) — one lean row per match, no wasted body.

Do not stop after one command. Chain impact → get → (symbol -r | grep verification) and read the key files via the `Read` tool once 1up has pointed you at them.
